
🚗 Indian Used Car Market Analysis
This project provides a comprehensive analysis of the Indian used car market, leveraging a dataset of over 4,000 vehicles. By utilizing SQL for data extraction and Python (Seaborn & Matplotlib) for visualization, the study identifies critical drivers of vehicle resale value across four key business dimensions.

1. The "Automatic" Premium (Asset Value)
File: avg_selling_price.sql
Metric: Average Price by Transmission Type.
Findings: There is a massive valuation gap between transmission types. Automatic vehicles command an average price of ₹1.4M, while Manual vehicles average just ₹400K.
Analysis: This 3.5x price difference indicates that automatics are predominantly found in newer, luxury-segment models, reflecting a high "convenience premium" in the Indian market.
2. The Depreciation Curve (Time-Series Trends)
File: year_over_year_depreciation.sql
Metric: Annual Value Loss via SQL Window Functions (LAG).
Findings: Vehicle values climbed steadily from 2011, peaking in 2019 at over ₹1M.
Analysis: The data highlights a critical "Depreciation Curve," where the first three years see the sharpest value loss. This provides a clear Buy vs. Sell signal: owners maximize returns by selling before year three, while value-seekers should target the 4–6 year window.
3. Market Ecosystem (Seller Distribution)
File: seller_type.sql
Metric: Volume by Seller Category.
Findings: The market is heavily decentralized, with Individual sellers dominating at 74.7%. Dealers account for 22.9%, while "Trustmark Dealers" (certified programs) represent only 2.4%.
Analysis: The high volume of peer-to-peer sales suggests a market driven by direct negotiation rather than standardized showroom pricing, emphasizing the need for independent vehicle inspections.
4. The Ownership Penalty (Risk & Pricing)
File: owner_impact.sql
Metric: Price Floors (MIN) and Ceilings (MAX) by Ownership History.
Findings: First-owner vehicles exhibit the highest price ceiling, reaching nearly ₹9M.
Analysis: Value decay is non-linear; as a vehicle moves to a Third or Fourth owner, the "price ceiling" collapses. This identifies the "Price Floor"—the fundamental residual value a vehicle retains regardless of its ownership history.
